CHECKS AND BALANCES

Definition

The idea that the power of the United States is split into three branches known as the executive branch, legislative branch, and judicial branch to prevent too much power being held by one group alone.

Evidence

An example of checks and balances being implemented in our constitution would be in article one. in article one section 9 clause 7 they state "No Money shall be drawn from the Treasury, but in Consequence of the Appropriations made by Law; and a regular Statement and Account of the Receipts and Expenditures of all public Money shall be published from time to time." This demonstrates that the government cannot spend money unless Congress has passed a law approving it. This is an example of checks and balances because this checks the power of the executive branch through allowing the legislative branch to control there spending.

INDEPENDENT JUDICIARY

Definition

A separate branch not pressured by society to make certain decisions that is also independent from the other branches so it can check our system using the constitution as guidance.

Evidence

Evidence of independent judiciary being implemented into the constitution would be in article 3. In article 3 section 2 clause 1 the constitution states "The judicial Power shall extend to all Cases, in Law and Equity, arising under this Constitution, the Laws of the United States, and Treaties made, or which shall be made, under their Authority; -to all Cases affecting Ambassadors, other public Ministers and Consuls; -to all Cases of admiralty and maritime Jurisdiction; -to Controversies between two or more States, --between Citizens of different States, -between Citizens of the same State claiming Lands under Grants of different States." This is an example of independent judiciary being implemented into the constitution because it states when judicial power can get involved. Also in the clause, it states that this is when the Supreme Court determined that it had the power to decided if a law is constitutional or not. Deciding if a law is constitutional or not is a direct example of independent judiciary because one of the ideas embedded into independent judiciary is that it uses the constitution as guidance to decide if our system is constitutional or not which demonstrates the principle of independent judiciary being implemented into our constitution.

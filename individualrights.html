INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS

Definition

Rights that are automatically given to the people of the United States because they are human and are pretected by the government.

Evidence

In the Bill Of Rights, we see individual rights being implemented into the constitution. In the Bill Of Rights the fifth amendment of the constitution states "No person shall be held to answer for a capital, or otherwise infamous crime, unless on a presentment or indictment of a Grand Jury, except in cases arising in the land or naval forces, or in the Militia, when in actual service in the time of War or public danger; nor shall any person be subject for the same offence to be twice put in jeopardy of life or limb; nor shall be compelled in any criminal case to be a witness against himself, nor be deprived of life, liberty, or property, without due process of law; nor shall private property be taken for public use, without just compensation." This shows individual rights being implemented into the constitution because it guarantees basic rights to all people accused of crimes. This amendment says that a person accused of a crime cannot be trialed more than once which prevents the government from prosecuting people unfairly, this is known as double jeopardy. This amendment also states that a person cannot be forced to give evidence against himself. There are also many other basic rights granted to all people accused of crimes in this amendment. This amendment is a direct example of individual rights being implemented into the constitution because it gives all people accused of crimes basic rights.

FEDERALISM

Definition

Promotes a robust federal government that shares its power with the states which are divided in three main ways; delegated power is power for the federal government only, concurrent power is the power shared with the federal government and the states, and reserved power is for the states and local government only and these are the three main ways the federal government shares its power.

Evidence

Some evidence of federalism being implemented into the constitution is in the first article. In the first article section 4 clause 1, the constitution states "The Times, Places and manner of holding Elections for Senators and Representives, shall be prescribed in each State by the Legislature thereof; but the Congress may at any time by Law make or alter such Regulations, except as to the Places of chusing Senators." This is evidence of federalism being implemented into the constitution because the constitution states that states can choose where and when they want to hold elections for the Senators and Representatives, but Congress may alter these regulations, except as to the places choosing senators which is an example of concurrent power. This is an example of concurrent power because both the federal government and the states can alter the regulations. So the states can technically choose the times the election is held, but the federal government can alter this if needed showing how the power is shared.

Topic Example

Federalism is sort of like the "!important" rule in CSS. For example, There are two F's below for federalism. One F has an "!important" rule on it, and the other doesn't. An "!important" rule is kind of like delegated power. Now let's pretend you're a state and you want to change the color of one of the F's too yellow. Please go to Chrome if you want to do the following steps. Let's try to change the F's color on the left too yellow first. This F has an "!important" rule on it, and you are a state so you can't overrule this because this is a delegated power only given to the federal government. To see this in action right click on the left F and click inspect. In the bottom right area, you should see element.style { }. Click on this and type "color" and then "return" on the keyboard. After that type "yellow" and then "return" on the keyboard again. As you can see the color didn't change because you are a state trying to make a law that only the federal government can make because the "!important" rule is like a delegated power. This shows how the federal government has power over all the states to make certain laws if its a delegated power. But as we know in federalism the power is shared with the government, states or people which means states can also make some of there own laws if this power is not given to the federal government or prohibited for them to have. So you are a state, and you want to change the color of the F to yellow on the right which doesn't have any federal law prohibiting you from doing this, and this power is not given to the federal government either. So in CSS this mean there is not an "!important" rule on the right F which means this is a reserved power for only the states. To change the color simple right click on the right F, click inspect and then in the lower right-hand corner click on element.style { }. Type "color" than "return" on the keyboard and then type "yellow" and then "return" on the keyboard. The F should be yellow because no federal law is preventing you from doing this and this power is not given to the federal government which demonstrates reserved power. Some power though is shared with both the states and federal government known as concurrent power. Now let's pretend that the first F is a concurrent power that is shared with both the federal government and the states and your still a state. So to demonstrate this right click on the first F and click inspect and than click on element.style { } and type "color" than "return" on the keyboard and then type "yellow" and after "yellow" hit "space" and than type "!important" and hit "return." Since the first F is now a concurrent power, you the state can change it even though there's an "!important rule on it because you share the power with the government. This demonstrates how power is divided and shared between the states and the federal government which outlines the principle of federalism.

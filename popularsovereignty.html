POPULAR SOVEREIGNTY

Definition

The power of the United States in held within the people of the United States which use their power in the form of voting, this is also known as direct democracy.

Evidence

Evidence of popular sovereignty being implemented into the constitution would be in article 2. In article 2 section 1 clause 2 the constitution states "Each State shall appoint, in such Manner as the Legislature thereof may direct, a Number of Electors, equal to the whole Number of Senators and Representatives to which the State may be entitled in the Congress: but no Senator or Representative, or Person holding an Office of Trust or Profit under the United States, shall be appointed to Elector." This section of the constitution establishes the electoral college. In the clause, it says each state chooses its electors to cast its vote for the president. We the people vote to choose the electors in our state. This is a direct example of popular sovereignty being implemented into the constitution because we the people have the power which we use by voting and we vote for our electors in our state which will vote for our president. This demonstrates how we the people vote for electors to vote for our president which demonstrates popular sovereignty.
